Determinism is a fairly important concept in Philosophy, and one of particular interset to me for various reasons. In this thought, I will explain my view of determinism, address some counterarguments against determinism, and finally give some reasons why I find determinism so important.

What is Determinism?

Determinism, at least to me, is the view that everything occurs for a reason. "Reason" is somewhat weak, so it should be more precisely put as the view that all events that occur in the world are determined by previously existing causes. I find the Standford Encyclopedia of Philosophy's Article by Carl Hoefer to have a good explanation, so I will do my best to summarize it.

According to Hoefer, a nearly all encompasing definition of determinism is as follows:

The universe is governed by (or is under the sway of) determinism if and only if, given a specified way things are at a time t, the way things go thereafter is fixed as a matter of natural law.

In order to gain a clearer concept of Determinism, it serves to further define and clarify the bolded terms.

What does "the universe" mean?

I think the most important part of including the whole universe is the claim that determinism applies to all things. Let's say for instance, that the definition changed to:

So evidently, there are issues with distilling determinism to the event level, but why exactly is it necessary to include the scope of the whole universe? Why not just take the Earth for example, as our starting point? The simple answer is that despite there being a speed limit upon the physical speed at which things can change (the speed of light), everything effects everything, in a sort of butterfly effect way. As an example, let's assume that you decide to go stargazing, and the light from some distant star strikes your retina and subtly influences the way you think. The state of the Earth at the time t did not determine that the light of the distant star would arrive and strike your retina. Then, it is obvious that the scope of determinism must encompass all that could influence us.
